# v5 — slider-shape channels + reverse sliders (17-ch)

**Purpose:** the v5 batch — dedicated slider-anchor channels + a `slides` channel to fix straight
sliders and recover reverse sliders, and the decision to **defer** style/mapper conditioning. |
**STATIC** (frozen). (Old RESEARCH §10.3, §10.5.)

Run detail + play feedback: [status/results.md](../status/results.md). Channel spec:
[knowledge/signal-encoding.md](../knowledge/signal-encoding.md).

## The two target complaints

The most visible, highest-confidence complaints: **sliders are straight lines (60–80%)** and **no
reverse sliders**. Validated direction: Mapperatorinator's `osu_diffusion` makes slider anchors +
repeat counts first-class typed points.

### Why the old encoding failed
Slider shape was traced into the shared `cursor_x/cursor_y` channels across the hold, so it competed
with the global cursor path and denoised to mush → the decoder's straight-vs-curve test usually picked
a line. And `slides` (repeat count) was dropped entirely: a reverse slider encoded as a slow forward
slider, so the decoder always emitted `slides=1`.

## The fix — "hold-box" anchor encoding (the F batch)

Move shape into **dedicated channels carrying control-point offsets as a constant value over the
slider's hold span** (like `slider_hold`, but valued). Hold-boxes are smooth and denoise-friendly;
decode reads the span mean. Fixed **K=3 anchor slots** (covers waves/arcs):
- For anchor *i*: `slider_dx_i`, `slider_dy_i` = the offset of control point *i* from the slider head,
  normalised by playfield size into ~[−1,1], held constant over the span (baseline 0 elsewhere).
- **`slides` channel**: hold-box valued by repeat count (`clip((slides−1)/3,0,1)*2−1`); decode rounds
  the span mean back.
- The cursor channels **stop** tracing slider control points (that was the noise source) — cursor
  keeps only the object head (+ slider end for flow continuity); slider body shape lives in the anchor
  channels.

**Channel layout 10 → 17** (new channels APPENDED, indices 0–9 unchanged): `…, 10 slider_dx1, 11
slider_dy1, 12 slider_dx2, 13 slider_dy2, 14 slider_dx3, 15 slider_dy3, 16 slides`. Appending keeps
v4/ranked 10-ch models + tests valid. **flip aug must negate `slider_dx_i` (h-flip) and `slider_dy_i`
(v-flip)**; `slides` is flip-invariant. Fresh train (17-ch can't load the 10-ch v4 ckpt).

## Style / mapper conditioning — DEFERRED (not in this batch)

KMeans cluster quality, whether a coarse label captures real "style", and whether conditioning on it
helps are all uncertain and indirect → too speculative to bundle. `CONTEXT_DIM` unchanged. Stays a
separate later experiment.

## Outcome

The two target complaints are **fixed**:
- **Curved sliders: 100% bezier** (3 control points) — was ~20–40% before. The dedicated anchor
  channels work.
- **Reverse sliders: 27%** — was **0** (impossible in 10-ch). (27% is *above* real ~8% → may
  over-produce reverses.)

**Play feedback (in-game):** kiai 9/10, reverse sliders good, streams way better. Fixes applied (no
retrain):
- **AR** was terrible (`4+0.7·sr`); now `target_settings` AR `7.75+0.25·sr` (AR9 ≈ median player) —
  also matches real ranked AR.
- **Straight vs curved mix** — v5 emitted *every* slider as a 3-point bezier ("line built from curve
  points"). Decode now emits a **linear** slider when the anchor polygon is near-collinear
  (`SLIDER_STRAIGHT_RATIO`); ~77% B / 23% L. (Round 2 `[AI-v5c]`: RDP-simplify decoded anchors to kill
  the "imposter line" — clustered redundant anchors on a straight slider.)
- `package_map` was overriding the generated AR/OD with the *original*'s (so all prior in-game tests
  ran at the original's AR) — now keeps the generated settings.

**Round 2 decode fixes (no retrain):** intro junk trim (drop a leading cluster before a big gap, not
just one note); merge overlapping spinners within 800 ms; `--timing-from <ref.osu>` for exact
BPM+offset on known songs.

## Still open after v5 (the v6+ backlog)

- **Rhythm** — ✅ off-¼ notes FIXED: the model places notes on 1/8 & 1/6 grids; `generate` now snaps
  to `divisors=(4,8,6)`. Remaining 1–2 s pauses are a *density* gap (model-side), not snap.
- **Hitsounds** below ranked level → gold-data + maybe an accent-density condition.
- **Slider velocity (SV)** — *ignored (everything SV=1)*; the right fix is a learned SV channel (→ v7).
- **Patterns** (some nasty) — flow / under-dispersion work (→ v7→v9).

## Gold-data filter (introduced here, used from v6)

`preprocess --gold` = `--ranked-only --require-kiai --single-bpm --min-hitsound-frac 0.1 --min-sr 1
--max-sr 10`. New manifest fields `n_uninherited` (BPM-change detection) + `hitsound_frac`. Removes the
weakest training signal (multi-BPM the model can't time, hitsound-sparse maps, extreme SR).
