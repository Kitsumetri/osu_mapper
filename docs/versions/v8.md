## The gap v8 targets

v7.5 **regresses to the *average* map for the conditioned SR**: on jump-spam songs it caps at
mean-spacing ~116 px / jump_ratio ~0.13 vs the song's 167 px / 0.39. Spacing is object *position*,
untouched by any decode lever → the headline remaining quality gap.

### Why absolute x/y under-disperses
Position is stored as **absolute** `cursor_x/y`. Under ε/v-MSE the model learns `E[x0 | x_t, cond]`;
for position the conditional mean ≈ playfield centre (a jump can go anywhere → the mean of a broad
distribution is the middle). Spacing is the **difference** of two mean-regressed positions → collapses
below even the real *average*. The spatial channels are worst-hit because (a) they are 2-of-21
channels with high-frequency content drowned by the easy piecewise channels, and (b) spacing is a
2nd-order statistic of a 1st-order target.

### The three-channel-collapse buckets (durable analysis)
Mean-regression pulls every channel toward its conditional mean; whether that hurts depends on what
the mean *is*. **A channel is endangered in proportion to how much its correct value depends on
information NOT in the audio.**
- **(1) Safe — the mean is the right answer (audio-determined):** `onset`, holds, hitsound *count*.
  The mel pins note *times* → rhythm is the model's best dimension.
- **(2) Collapse — a free creative choice the audio doesn't pin down (P4-B's family):** `cursor_x/y` →
  centre → spacing collapses; slider anchors → collinear → straight; `corner` (rare binary) →
  base-rate → thresholded to ~2%.
- **(3) Hurt by a different mechanism:** `kiai` (structural, boundary-ambiguous → flips per sample →
  needs the supervised head); `spinner` (under-produced from data sparsity).
Bucket 2 is the single biggest gap and the shared cause of *both* the pattern and straight-slider
complaints.

### Key correction: signed Δ does NOT fix it
Signed Δx/Δy mean-regress to ≈0 (jump directions are ~uniform → mean displacement cancels). What
survives mean-regression is a **non-negative magnitude**: spacing *distance* has a conditional mean ≈
the true typical spacing (a large positive number, no cancellation). Evidence: the **curve cue
worked** (a non-negative scalar); the **corner cue under-fired** (a rare binary). → **encode dense
positive scalars, not sparse binaries.**

## v8 design — the spacing-magnitude channel (`CH_SPACING`=20, 21-ch)

- **Encode:** for each hit object, `s = dist(prev object's end pos, this object's start pos)`
  (release→press = what the player's aim traverses). Store `s / SPACING_PX_SCALE` (≈256–300) clipped
  to [0,~1.2], **held over the inter-object gap**, baseline 0. **Flip-aug invariant** (distance is
  unchanged by mirror — no negation needed). Index-appended → old ckpts still load.
- **Decode (`respace_by_magnitude`):** rebuild positions by accumulating displacement = (model's own
  direction) × (channel magnitude), re-anchored per new-combo, **reflecting** at playfield walls
  (preserves magnitude, unlike clamping). Decode-tunable via `--spacing-scale` α (α=0 = today's
  behaviour).
- **Complementary lever — per-channel loss up-weighting** (`--spatial-loss-weight`): weight
  cursor/anchors/spacing/**corner** above the easy piecewise channels in the loss (renormalised to
  mean 1). Counters the "2-of-21 underfit" mechanism + corner's under-fire. (Corner stays binary —
  count re-encode was REJECTED: a lower encoded value clears the decode threshold *less* often → worse
  under-fire.)

**Scope decision:** v8 = spacing-magnitude channel (cursor) + spatial loss up-weighting on
cursor/anchors/spacing/corner, one reprocess → `ranked-v8` (21-ch), one train. A richer slider-shape
representation is deferred to v9. Attribution holds because the metrics are disjoint *and* the spacing
effect is decode-tunable (α=0 isolates the loss-weighting at eval, no retrain).

## Outcome — partial; the channel regresses to the SR-average

Trained base-160 (val 0.041, clean). **The core bet only half held.**
- ✅ **base-160 is unblocked** — clean 60 epochs through the e12–21 divergence zone that killed v2/v3
  (ε-pred). **v-pred + zero-SNR is the unblock**; `--compile` works at base-160. Bigger models are now
  trainable — a durable capability win independent of the spacing outcome.
- ✅ **No regression** (curves 0.369 ≈ real 0.38, SV intact).
- ❌ **The spacing channel does NOT auto-adapt per-song.** On the Happppy jump song (real spacing 173 /
  jump 0.42) the channel predicts only ~120–127 px — it mean-regresses to `E[spacing | audio, SR]` =
  the **SR-average**, NOT the per-song extreme. **Why §10.11's prediction was wrong:** a magnitude
  scalar *does* mean-regress to the *correct* value — but "correct" is the SR *average*, and the
  channel **shares the cursor's audio+SR conditioning**, so it has no extra per-song info. Both → the
  same SR-average. The curve cue worked only because decode *forces* a bow; respace faithfully
  reproduces the channel's compressed magnitude.
- ⚠️ **`--spacing-scale >1` is a manual global jump dial** (Happppy raw 0.116 → 2.5 → 0.297) but
  **HURTS in-game** (play feedback): relocated objects read/play worse (flow/readability). **→ use
  `--spacing-scale 0` (raw); respace shelved.** SR conditioning already gives per-song/per-SR spacing
  variation (Kano sr5→sr7 raw spacing 123→169, jump 0.09→0.30).

**Verdict:** net **+** over v7.5 (stable bigger base + no regression), but it did **not** break the
jump ceiling *automatically*. **The real per-song fix (→ v9):** condition on an **audio-inferred
aim-intensity / target spacing** (computed per-song from onset-energy/spectral-flux, fed like
`--density`). A passive channel can't beat the conditioning it shares — the lever must be *new
information at the input*, or an objective that samples extremes. See [v9.md](v9.md).

## Carried-forward design: the timing model (BPM + offset for novel songs)

Not shipped — the foundation (`src/timing_model/`: `labels.py` + `metrics.py` + 6 tests) is done on
CPU; benchmarking/training are pending. Replaces the librosa estimate (~28% phase-exact) for songs with
no reference `.osu`; `--timing-from` stays the exact path for known songs.

### The problem
osu! timing = an uninherited (red) point `(time, beat_length, meter)`. We need **tempo** (BPM),
**phase/offset** (the ms of the anchor downbeat; needs ~5–10 ms accuracy or every object drifts), and
**downbeat** (which beat is bar-1). Gold maps are single-BPM (filter to single-BPM + meter 4 for v1);
variable-BPM (~26%) is a later extension.

### Our unfair advantage
The corpus ships **human-verified BPM+offset+meter** in every map → a free, large, in-distribution
(anime/electronic/J-core, often 180–250 BPM) benchmark *and* training set where general trackers
(trained on Ballroom/GTZAN) may be out of distribution. Split by `audio_id` (difficulties share audio).

### SOTA survey (2024–25)
- **Beat This!** (Foscarin et al., ISMIR 2024) — transformer, SOTA, no DBN post-processing; the
  reference arch (mel front-end, wide-tolerance framewise targets, heavy tempo-stretch aug).
- **Beat Transformer** (2022); **BEAST** (2023, online streaming); **madmom** RNN/TCN + DBN
  bar-pointer (strong but Python-3.12/Windows install pain → benchmark last); **BeatNet** CRNN +
  particle filter; **librosa** = the current ~28%-exact baseline.

### Recommended architecture
Two routes (reuse our mel pipeline + RoPE + 1D-conv infra): a **small transformer** (Beat This!-style,
generalises best, data-hungry) or a **pragmatic TCN** (Davies & Böck 2019, lighter — the recommended
first bespoke model). Input log-mel at a finer hop (~100 fps / 10 ms) + sub-frame parabolic peak
interpolation. Targets: two widened-window framewise activations (beat, downbeat); loss
positive-weighted/focal BCE, shift-tolerant. Augmentation (tempo-stretch) is load-bearing.

### Inference — global grid fit
For single-BPM: tempogram → BPM candidates; phase-align each by cross-correlating a click train with
the activation; pick best; take the offset on a downbeat and refine sub-frame. "Super timing":
ensemble over windows/passes → median. A neat self-consistent fallback: fit a grid to *our own*
generated onset channel.

### Correctness checking
`mir_eval.beat` (F-measure, AMLt/AMLc tolerate octave errors) + **osu exact-match** (BPM within 0.1,
counting octave errors separately; offset error mod beat_length bucketed <5 ms excellent / <10 good /
<20 playable; whole-song grid drift). Test split by `audio_id`.

### Plan
1. ✅ Dataset extractor + eval harness done (`src/timing_model/`, CPU).
2. Benchmark pretrained (Beat This! → BeatNet → librosa; madmom last) on the corpus → may make
   training unnecessary.
3. Train bespoke only if pretrained falls short — TCN first, transformer (+ RoPE) for upside.
